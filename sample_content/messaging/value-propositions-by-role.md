# Fourth Value Propositions by Role

## Executive Summary

Different stakeholders in a restaurant or hospitality organization care about different outcomes. This document maps Fourth's value to each key buyer persona, with quantified impact, proof points, and discovery questions.

---

## CFO / Chief Financial Officer

### Primary Concerns
- Labor cost as % of revenue (typically 25-35% in restaurants)
- Payroll accuracy and compliance
- Audit and legal risk exposure
- Predictable technology costs
- Food cost control and inventory accuracy

### Fourth's Value

**Quantified Impact:**
- **2-4% labor cost reduction** through AI-optimized scheduling aligned to demand (Fourth iQ)
- **50% reduction** in payroll errors through automated calculations (tips, overtime, split shifts)
- **$50K-$200K annual savings** in compliance penalty avoidance per 100 locations
- **3-5 vendors consolidated** into one platform, reducing total technology spend by $800K+ (400-location operator)
- **2-5% food cost savings** through MacromatiX inventory management and waste tracking

**Key Proof Points:**
- **Noodles & Company**: $4M annual savings through labor optimization and food cost control
- **National QSR (2,500 locations)**: Reduced labor costs by 3.2% in first year, saving $12M annually
- **Regional casual dining (180 locations)**: Eliminated $340K in compliance penalties within 6 months
- **Fast casual (400 locations)**: Consolidated 6 vendors to 1, saving $800K in annual software costs

**Discovery Questions:**
1. "What's your current labor cost as a percentage of revenue?"
2. "How many payroll corrections do you process per pay period?"
3. "Have you had any scheduling compliance penalties in the past 2 years?"
4. "How many workforce management vendors are you paying today?"
5. "What's your actual vs. theoretical food cost variance?"
